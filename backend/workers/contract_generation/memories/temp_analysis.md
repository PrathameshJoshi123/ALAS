## INDIAN CONTRACT ACT MAPPING (INTERNAL)

### **Section 10: What Agreements are Contracts**
- **Relevance**: Ensures the supply agreement is legally enforceable as it meets the criteria of free consent, lawful consideration, and lawful object.
- **Alignment**: The template aligns with this section as it includes mutual obligations, consideration (payment for goods), and lawful purpose.
- **Implications**: The contract must explicitly state the consideration (price per unit) and ensure both parties are competent and consent freely.

### **Section 27: Agreement in Restraint of Trade**
- **Relevance**: Ensures no clause restrains either party from lawful trade post-termination, except as permitted by exceptions.
- **Alignment**: The template does not include any restraint of trade clauses, which is compliant.
- **Implications**: Any non-compete or exclusivity clause must be reasonable and limited to the contract period.

### **Section 37: Obligation of Parties to Contracts**
- **Relevance**: Mandates that both parties must perform their obligations as agreed.
- **Alignment**: The template includes obligations for both Supplier (delivery, quality) and Buyer (payment, inspection).
- **Implications**: The contract must clearly define obligations for both parties to avoid disputes.

### **Section 56: Agreement to Do Impossible Act**
- **Relevance**: Ensures that obligations are feasible and not impossible to perform.
- **Alignment**: The template does not include impossible acts, but Force Majeure (Clause 2.47) covers unforeseen impossibility.
- **Implications**: The contract should include a Force Majeure clause to address unforeseen events.

### **Section 73: Compensation for Loss or Damage Caused by Breach of Contract**
- **Relevance**: Governs compensation for breach, including direct and indirect losses.
- **Alignment**: The template includes default and termination clauses (Clause 2.41, 2.43) but lacks explicit compensation details.
- **Implications**: The contract must specify penalties for delay, breach, and termination to comply with this section.

### **Section 124-147: Indemnity and Guarantee**
- **Relevance**: Covers indemnification and liability, which are critical for supply agreements.
- **Alignment**: The template includes indemnity clauses (Clause 2.38) but needs alignment with Indian law principles.
- **Implications**: The contract must clearly define indemnification scope and limitations to comply with Indian law.
